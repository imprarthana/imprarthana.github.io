Dry Fruits for babies in winters: Right way of feeding

How are dry fruits made?

Fresh fruits are easily perishable. In order to avoid the perishability of fresh fruits they are dried. In dried fruits, the content of water is reduced to 18%. This removal of water from fresh fruits enables them to be stored in ordinary conditions and increases their shelf life. The nutritional content fruit of fresh such as fibers, carbohydrates, minerals, and vitamin A remains intact even after dehydration. On the other hand, nuts are also dried fruit with an edible oily kernel. The enormous healing properties and nutrition that these nuts offer make them a must addition to the diet of kids. Dried fruits are among the healthiest dietary alternatives to foods containing refined sugar (sweets, candies, jams, fruit preserves) and are an excellent means to satisfy the desire to eat sweet things.

The nutritional content of dry fruits

Dry fruits are filled with nutrients and fats that act as an immediate supplier of energy to the body. The perfect blend of nutrition and energy makes dry fruits and nuts a popular choice among mothers. Nuts are packed with fiber, vitamins such as folic acid, niacin, vitamin E, and B6, and minerals such as calcium, magnesium, potassium, selenium, and copper. Dry fruits contain protein whereas nuts are rich in unsaturated fats. Dry fruits, like fresh ones, have very low sodium content and high dietary fiber. Traditional dried fruits do not contain added sugar. They contain sugar in the form of glucose and fructose.

Health benefits of dry fruits

Epidemiological studies conducted on children have shown a significant positive correlation between dry fruit consumption and overall health. Here are some of the dry fruits which must be added to your kid’s diet:
1.Loaded with natural sugars, raisins can be given as candies to kids. It promotes growth and weight gain. Rich in iron, they are recommended for individuals with anemia.
2.Figs show laxative properties by improving bowel movements, thereby preventing constipation. It also boosts a baby's immune system and supports a healthy liver.
3.Munakka is helpful in aiding digestion. An adequate amount of calcium present in it helps strengthen the bones.
4.Almonds are effective in reducing chronic coronary heart risk and maintaining blood hemoglobin levels.
5.Walnuts show more anti-inflammatory effects than other nuts. They have a significantly high concentration of DHA, a type of Omega-3 which is known to protect brain health in newborns.
6.Cashews are a rich source of healthy fats that potentially affects a baby's nervous system and brain development. They also contain zinc and fiber, two nutrients that are important for healthy growth and digestion.

Ways of consuming dry fruits

It is recommended to soak almonds, walnuts, figs, raisins, and munakka overnight and consume them after removing their peel. Soaking them helps in eliminating phytic acid and dissolving unwanted minerals and vitamins in water, thereby increasing the availability of required nutrients and better digestibility of the nuts and dried fruits. Almonds and cashews can also be roasted to enhance the taste and served as snacks.

The right time to eat dry fruits

Dry fruits and nuts can be eaten any time throughout the day but in order to fetch maximum benefits, overnight-soaked dried fruits should be consumed empty stomach before breakfast. Roasted and salted nuts can also be enjoyed as an evening snack as they provide ample energy.

How many dry fruits should kids consume?

Toddlers can be fed with finely ground nuts but avoid giving them raisins as they may stick in their throat. Kids more than 2 years of age can be given 2-3 nuts and dry fruits. Each dietician recommends “a handful” of dried fruits in the diet of children more than 7 years of age with a view to healthy nutrition.
