Mustard Oil: Types, Uses, and Benefits

Mustard Oil, popularly known as Sarson ka tel, has been traditionally used in Indian households. A common ingredient in Indian cuisine, it is considered to be one of the best edible oils for cooking. Pure mustard oil has a high smoke point of around 250°C, putting it on par with other fats like butter. This makes it a common choice for high-heat cooking methods like frying, roasting, baking, and grilling. It has the lowest fatty acid content among all the edible oils, making it heart-friendly. Known for its versatility, mustard oil can be used as a culinary, therapeutic, or essential oil. The two main varieties of mustard oil are yellow and black.

Black Mustard Oil

Natively grown in India, black mustard oil is best used for Indian dishes. It has a distinctive strong pungent aroma that enhances the taste of the dishes to which it is added. It is dark brown to black. Due to its high smoke point, it is ideal for sauteing, deep-frying, etc. This oil is also utilized for the production of soap and medicinal remedies. It is a great choice for Ayurvedic practices like oil pulling, etc. Black mustard contains chemicals that might initially reduce pain when applied to the skin. It is used for the common cold, painful joints and muscles (rheumatism), and arthritis.

Yellow Mustard Oil

The origin of yellow mustard can be traced to the East Mediterranean region. Yellow mustard oil or American mustard is widely used in the US. It comes with a mild flavor and maintains the original taste of the dish. The yellow mustard oil is very less pungent in comparison to regular mustard oil. It has more protein content than black mustard oil. It can be used even as a replacement for refined oil and pure ghee. It is widely used in hot dogs, sandwiches, and hamburgers and added to oil, vinegar, or honey for making marinades and dressings. Black mustard oil being less expensive than yellow mustard oil is more commonly seen in Indian kitchens.

Nutritional Content and Health Benefits of Mustard Oil

Mustard oil is a potentially rich source of Vitamin E which is a strong antioxidant, vitamin A which is helpful for the normal vision of the eyes, and vitamin C which serves as a coenzyme for metabolic functions. It has protein and zinc for growth and development of the body, calcium is helpful for bone health, and selenium for liver and muscular health. Additionally, it has dietary fibers which help to stimulate the digestive functions in our body. About 100 gm of mustard oil contains 884 calories which is a source of energy. Its optimum ratio of omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids provides nutrition and boosts immunity. The high amount of alpha-linolenic acid present in mustard oil helps to control the high cholesterol level and cardiovascular disease. This oil serves antibiotic, fungicidal, and anti-carcinogenic properties and reduces the effect of chronic diseases. Due to the absence of trans fat, this oil also helps in maintaining the human body temperature and reduces the chances of diabetes. It promotes appetite by stimulating the flow of salivary and gastric juices.

Uses of Mustard Oil

Mustard oil has been used for the treatment of various skin diseases and wounds and its medicinal properties are attributed to the isothiocyanate. It has been used internally and externally since ancient times. Mustard and its oil have been used as a foot bath for aching feet and in the form of plasters over the back and chest to treat bronchitis and pneumonia. Internally, mustard seeds have been used as appetite stimulants and diuretics. Owing to its pungent nature, mustard oil has been used to relieve colds and coughs for decades. The paste of mustard oil and turmeric is used for healthy teeth. It is also used for rub-downs and massages, thought to improve blood circulation, muscular development, and skin texture. It is a great option for skincare, haircare, and your or your baby’s massage. It may be used as a natural sunscreen since it ensures protection against harmful UV rays. This oil may help heal skin rashes, allergies, and infections. The mustard oil is also a good hair tonic, stimulates hair growth, helps relieve dandruff and itchy scalp, and may prevent premature hair graying. It also acts as a preservative by making a thick layer on the pickle which resists the entry of microbes.
