Benefits of eating Locally grown food

What does it mean to eat locally?

You must have been advised to eat local food by your dietician. The benefits of eating local food are plenty. Local food has gained popularity in recent years and with good reason but do you know what exactly is local food? Local food consists of produce and other foods grown on the farm of your local community. The “localness” of food has been defined in many different ways. For the sake of simplicity, the geographical definition of food products coming from a 160–400 km radius of being sold or the consumer’s home is often used to define local food.

Why eat local food?

Concerns about health and the environment have put locally grown products in the spotlight across the globe. There are many benefits to buying locally grown food, and each person makes their choice for different reasons. Locally grown food paves way for important economic opportunities, provides health benefits, and helps to reduce negative impacts on the environment. It also helps bring the community together and gives people the opportunity to make a difference.

Health benefits of locally grown food

Nutritional content: Local food comes to your home just after the harvest or within some hours of harvest hence it is less likely that the nutrient value of fruits and veggies decreases. As soon as a food is harvested its nutrient content begins to deteriorate, specifically vitamins C, E, A, and some B. Some foods, especially fruits and veggies, lose nutrients during transportation and storage, making local options usually more nutritious. Therefore, local food improves your heart, blood, brain gastrointestinal/digestion, and vision health.


Fresh food: When grown locally, the crops are picked at their peak of ripeness. On the other hand, non-local produce is harvested early in order to be shipped and distributed to your local retail store. Food that’s sourced from local farms doesn’t need to be shipped from afar and is very fresh, especially when it comes to production. At the same time, fresh food also fills your mouth with taste.


Safer Consumption: The chances are less of local food getting contaminated since there are fewer steps involved between food being harvested and coming to your table. It ensures that you are consuming food that has passed the highest safety standards. Local food does not travel far so there is no need to use any preservatives to keep it fresh. Apart from this, you can always connect with the local producers to know how the crop was grown.


Seasonal food: Local food caters to your body’s seasonal needs. The cycle of seasonal produce is perfectly designed to support your health. For instance, the people who live in a seaside place, their diet consists of coconuts, which is beneficial to their health and helps them adjust to weather changes that occur there. Similarly, residents in Rajasthan like eating bajra roti because it minimizes thirst.

Environmental benefits of locally grown food

Less pollution: Local food doesn’t have to travel as far to arrive on your plate, so it helps reduce greenhouse gas emissions and contributes to improving our carbon footprint.


Less waste and plastic packaging: Local foods yield less pollution and waste due to their minimal or complete lack of packaging, processing, and transportation. Also, you can always bring your cloth bag to carry the veggies back home.


Healthier ecosystem: Supporting local farmers helps maintain green spaces and farmland in your area. Local farms that use sustainable practices may boost biodiversity, protect pollinators that are vital to healthy ecosystems, and promote clean air, water, and soil. Additionally, farmland attracts other types of biodiversity and gives animals, insects, and birds a place to live and thrive.


Sustainable Agriculture: Eating locally encourages sustainable agriculture since it involves the diversification of crop variety. Single crops grown over wide area results in the deterioration of soil and as a result the nutrient content of food also decreases.

Economic benefits of locally grown food

Supports local economy: Supporting local farmers and other producers, generates employment and benefits the local economy. Money spent on buying local foods continues to remain in your local economy only, creating local jobs and strong community relations. Also, since the food itself moves through fewer hands and travels less distance, more of the money you spend will end up in raising and growing those foods instead of their packaging and transportation.

Bottom Line

The greatest advantage of eating local food is that it keeps you connected to your roots. Consuming local food helps remove the detachment from farming that has been created in the modern world. It makes you aware of the best farm practices and how your food is grown. If you choose to purchase locally you are ensuring that there will be more local farms in your community tomorrow. So next time when you go shopping for groceries, buy local.
