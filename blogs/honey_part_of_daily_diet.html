Why honey should be a part of your daily diet?

Adding natural honey to your regular diet may improve various aspects of your health. Honey is a natural sweetener of high nutritional value and immense health benefits. The use of honey as a food and potential therapeutic agent has been known to mankind since time immemorial. It is rich in carbohydrates, mainly fructose and glucose. Besides, other vitamins and minerals are found in traces. Owing to the presence of high carbohydrate content, honey is an excellent alternative to refined sugar. In addition to that, it is also an exceptional source of energy.

Benefits of honey

Honey has been a popular ingredient due to its antimicrobial, antiviral, antiparasitic, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antimutagenic, and antitumor properties. Some of the benefits of this natural sweetener are listed below.


Natural sweetener: Honey has many advantages over refined sugar. It is sweeter, provides more energy, and is easy to digest.


Safe for diabetic patients: Unlike refined sugar, honey can be safely consumed and well tolerated by diabetic patients. It causes a significant decrease in blood glucose levels in the body due to its phytochemical constituents.


Used as an antiseptic: Honey has antiseptic properties. It is potent in treating burns, infected surgical wounds, and ulcers. It is used to treat eye diseases by topical application of undiluted honey to the affected eyes.


Promotes respiratory health: Honey is commonly used to treat inflammation in the bronchial tubes and cough. It is known to reduce asthma-related symptoms.


Improves oral health: The use of honey can promote oral health and wellness. Due to its antibacterial activity, honey has the potential to reduce the risk of dental caries.


Gastroprotective agent: Honey potentially inhibits the growth of the bacteria that causes peptic ulcers and gastritis in the respiratory tract. The application of honey for the prevention and treatment of gastrointestinal disorders is well known.

How not to have honey?

Honey is still a type of sugar, therefore it should be consumed in moderation only and should not exceed the limits. There are a few things that have to be kept in mind while having honey. Heating and cooking honey is not advisable as it may negatively affect its properties and alter its chemical composition thereby making it toxic. When overheated, the nutrients and enzymes of honey are destroyed, its color darkens and it develops a bitter taste. Apart from this, honey consumption is not recommended for infants below 12 months. There are chances that honey may contain a bacteria called Clostridium botulinum which causes a rare but severe type of food poisoning called botulism in infants. It may even result in paralysis.

Best ways to consume honey

Experts suggest that the consumption of raw honey is most beneficial for the body. Honey can be mixed with milk or water, but make sure that the liquid is cooled down before you mix honey in it. It can be enjoyed in the form of tea and other beverages to cure ailments such as colds and coughs. It can also be used as dressings and toppings in some dishes, salads, and shakes.
